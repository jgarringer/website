Fees

  • The fee for a standard 8 1/2 x 11 black and white document is 10 cents to 25 cents per page.
  • Postage fees may not exceed the actual cost of mailing
  • If an Agency offers enhanced electronic access it can establish user fees that must be approved by the Office of Open Records.
  • An Agency cannot charge for the time it takes to redact a document or the legal review needed to determine if a document is a public record.
  • An Agency may require pre-payment if the fees are expected to exceed $100.

Feral/Stray Cats

The Borough is participating with services available through the Cherished Cats Rescue Alliance Feral Cat Program to neuter/spay feral cats. The Borough would be willing to pay the cost of $40.00 per cat. The Borough is in need of volunteers (who are willing to trap the feral cats, drop them off at the appropriate time and place of the clinic, and care for them after the procedure). Cherrished Cats Rescue Alliance will contact the Borough office with available dates of clinics in the area (past locations were Bloomsburg, Berwick, and Selinsgrove). The Borough would then contact the list of volunteer residents and coordinate the information needed to participate. The Trap-Neuter-Return (TNR) has been proven to be the only humane and effective method to manage feral cat colonies. Contact the Borough office at 570-743-7565 if you would like to participate in this program.
Humane Traps: To trap a stray cat, bait the trap with canned cat food. Place the trap in an area that's sheltered against the weather. You may hear some alarming noises when the cat realizes he's trapped. Immediately cover the trap with a sheet, blanket or towel large enough to cover the entire trap; this will calm the cat considerably and prevent him from injuring himself in the trap. If the cat is confined with no access to water or shelter from inclement weather or predators, it's important that you remove the trap from the area immediately. Using gloves to prevent scrathes or bites, put the trap in a protected area until your appointment (which should be as soon as possible).
Beckoning Cat Project: Beckoning Cat Project is a non-profit organization whose mission statement is finding community based solutions for cats. First and foremost, this means providing a means for pet owners and caretakers to affordably spay or neuter their cats to stop the over population problem.

The Borough has addressed the issue of feral/stray cats through PA Pets as mentioned in previous newsletters. This is an ongoing issue and a successful program hinges on the participation of residents throughout the Borough. Feeding these animals is a bad idea and should be avoided at all cost. The following are some reminders to help curb the problem:

  • Refrain from feeding
  • Feeding and harboring leads to ownership responsibilities
  • Get involved in the spay/neuter program that the Borough currently funds and endorses
  • Be a responsible neighbor to reduce the numbers in your immediate area

    • The following are ways to deter stray cats from your landscaping:

    • Coffee Grounds
    • Citrus Peels
    • Dried Herbs
